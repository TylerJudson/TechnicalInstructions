Adding Missing Tags for Item Sorting in Fallout 4: An Instructional Guide

Introduction

These instructions are designed for Fallout 4 players who need to fix the missing-tag sorting bug that commonly appears in modded load orders. This bug can cause both vanilla and modded items to appear in incorrect categories, making inventory management difficult and reducing the effectiveness of sorting mods like FallUI Item Sorter. The intention of these instructions is to teach how to patch the user’s load order in a way that allows FallUI Sorter to sort all items added or changed in Fallout 4 by their mod list. Successfully completing these instructions requires a basic understanding of how your load order and plugins interact, as well as familiarity with Mod Organizer 2 and FO4Edit.

Before beginning, ensure that all required modding tools (found in the section Equipment and Materials) are already installed and properly configured. This guide teaches you how to patch your load order so that FallUI Item Sorter correctly categorizes all items added or modified by your installed plugins. The following sections will highlight the equipment and materials needed, the completed expected result, the instructions, and a troubleshooting guide.

  • Fallout 4 game

After completing these instructions, the bug that overwrites item tags will be resolved. Your inventory will load correctly, and all items, both vanilla and modded, will sort into the appropriate FallUI Sorter categories. Instructions

  1. Launch Mod Organizer 2
    1. Once MO2 is open, ensure that all checkboxes on the right-hand side are selected (Figure 3).
      Tip: Each checkbox represents a loaded plugin in your load order. Make sure all boxes are checked so that FO4Edit can access every plugin.
      Figure 3
      Mod Organizer 2 main window with all boxes checked
    2. Click on the large drop-down box to the left of the Run button. Then select <Edit..> (Figure 4).
      Figure 4
      Location of in MO2 for editing executables
    3. Create a new executable by pressing the "plus" sign and then selecting Add from file (Figure 5).
      Figure 5
      Adding executable by file in MO2
    4. Navigate to the file FO4Edit.exe in your Downloads folder. Select it and click "Open", then close the window by pressing "OK" (Figure 6).
      Note: If you cannot find the exe file, it may not have been downloaded correctly. You can download it from the link provided in the Equipment and Materials section.
      Figure 6
      Location of FO4Edit.exe in FO4Edit file
    5. Press the Run button (Figure 7). FO4Edit will open automatically.
      Note: If FO4Edit does not launch, close MO2, verify that the FO4Edit.exe path is correct, and try running it again.
      Figure 7
      Location for run button to execute FO4Edit executable
  2. When FO4Edit opens, three pop-ups will appear:
    1. Click "OK" on the first pop-up regarding patch notes (Figure 8).
      Figure 8
      Patch notes pop up
    2. Click "Close" on the second pop-up about the authors (Figure 9).
      Note: If the Close button is unresponsive, scroll to the bottom of the "Please Read" box and try again.
      Figure 9
      Read me for authors pop up
    3. The third pop-up (Figure 10) displays all loaded plugins. Ensure that all plugins are selected, then press "OK".
      Tip: You only need to select the mods you intend to modify. Selecting more plugins will increase load time.
      Figure 10
      Pop up for mod selection
    1. Click on the search bar labeled "Filter by filename" located in the bottom-left corner of the FO4Edit window.
    2. Type MojaveImports.esp into the search bar to locate the plugin (Figure 11).
      Figure 11
      Pop up for mod selection
    3. Click on MojaveImports.esp to expand its list of records.
    1. Press on the category "Ingestible" to expand its items.
    2. Click the item Ghoul Flesh. This will open a window on the right showing its editable attributes (Figure 12).
      Tip: The columns represent different plugins, listed across the top row of the table.
      Figure 12
      Clicking on mod to add tags
    3. Locate the row labeled FULL – Name. This row displays the sorting tag. For example, food items that inflict radiation damage use the tag [RadFood].
    1. Right-click the cell labeled FULL – Name and select Edit.
      Warning: Do not change any attribute other than FULL – Name. Editing unrelated fields (e.g., Script, Magic Effects, or Stats) can break the item.
    2. Press "Yes, I'm absolutely sure" on the pop-up warning about uninformed editing (Figure 13).
      Note: The changes you will make are minor and affect only the inventory sorting system. They will not create any harmful or irreversible effects.
      Figure 13
      Right-clicking on mod to add tags
    3. Locate the sorting tag in the FULL – Name row. The tag appears inside [] (Figure 14).
      Tip: This tag typically appears in the second column from the right.
    4. Add the tag to the right-most column by inserting it at the beginning of the text in that cell (e.g., [RadFood] for radioactive food items).
      Tip: Always include the [] brackets when adding a tag.
      Warning: To prevent the tag from being overwritten, the right-most column must always contain a tag.
      Figure 14
      Copying tag to another column
    5. Press Enter to save the tag.
  6. Identify and fix all items that are missing sorting tags.
    1. Open each category one at a time and scan its entries for items that are missing tags.
      Tip: Items missing tags are often highlighted in red.
      Note: Only physical items require tags. To confirm this, check whether the item has a FULL – Name attribute in its description. If it does not, you can skip it. Categories like Spells or Object Effect often contain non-physical entries.
    2. Click an item to display its attributes in the right-hand panel.
    3. If the item is missing a tag, follow the steps in Step 5 to add the correct tag to the right-most column.
      Tip: Only tag the items you intend to modify. Prioritize items you commonly use or that cause sorting problems.
  7. Once you have finished adding tags to all desired items, click on the three horizontal lines in the top-left corner of FO4Edit, then select Save.
  8. Boot up Fallout 4 and check your inventory to confirm that all items are now sorting into their correct categories.

Troubleshooting Guide

These instructions assume you have already installed FO4Edit and have basic familiarity with mod management through MO2. If you are new to modding, it is recommended to back up your save files before proceeding.

The process of adding tags can be time-consuming depending on how many mods you have installed. Plan to spend 30-60 minutes if you have a large mod list. You may want to focus on frequently used items first.

Some mods may already have proper tags from other patches or updates. Always check existing tags before making changes to avoid conflicts or duplicates in your load order.

If you make a mistake while editing, you can close FO4Edit without saving and restart the process. FO4Edit will not modify your files until you explicitly save them in step 7.
